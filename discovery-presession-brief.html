The Purpose of This Session

Before any AI tool gets built, we need to deeply understand how your business operates today. This discovery session is our structured way of doing that — together, as a team, before a single line of code is written.

Think of it as a working conversation, not a presentation. We'll ask questions about how your team currently gets information out of your JD Edwards system, where the friction is, and what "better" actually looks like to the people doing the work.

The goal is simple: We walk away with a shared, documented picture of your current state — the baseline we'll measure against when the POC delivers results. The more honest and specific your team can be, the stronger the proof of value will be at the end.
02
Who Should Be in the Room

This session works best with a small, focused group — typically 4 to 6 people. Here's the breakdown of who we're looking for and why each role matters:

👔 Executive Sponsor Required
Sets the strategic context. Helps the team understand why this project matters and what a successful outcome looks like at the leadership level. Doesn't need to stay for the full session — the first 20 minutes is the most important.
e.g., Chad Logan or equivalent decision maker
💼 Data / Process Owner Required
The person who currently fields data requests, runs reports, or owns the operational questions the AI chatbot will answer. This is often a finance, ops, or sales lead. The single most valuable person in the room.
e.g., Finance manager, Sales ops lead, Operations supervisor
🖥️ JDE / IT Administrator Required
Knows the JD Edwards configuration — which modules are active, any custom fields, and how data is structured. We won't ask for system access in this session, but we will ask questions that require this context.
e.g., JDE admin, DBA, or IT lead who supports the ERP
🔍 End User / Day-to-Day Requester Recommended
Someone who regularly asks for data or reports today — and who would actually use the chatbot. Their perspective on what questions get asked most often is invaluable for scoping the POC correctly.
e.g., Sales rep, dispatcher, accountant, ops coordinator
Who to leave out: Legal, procurement, and approvals-only stakeholders are better suited for a later review. Their presence in discovery sessions tends to shift the conversation toward risk rather than requirements.
03
How We'll Spend Our Time

The session typically runs 90 to 120 minutes. Here's the rough flow:

0–15 min
Context Setting & Goals
ThinkWork walks through the POC scope, confirms the SOW deliverables, and aligns on what success looks like from both sides.
15–45 min
Current State Capture
We map the current process: how data gets requested today, who handles it, how long it takes, and what manual workarounds exist. This becomes the baseline we measure against.
45–75 min
Use Case Scoping
We walk through the specific query types the chatbot should handle — prioritizing by frequency and business impact. This drives the UAT test cases for the POC sign-off.
75–90 min
KPI & Measurement Agreement
We agree on how we'll measure whether the POC worked — time saved, volume handled, accuracy thresholds. These become the formal success criteria.
90–120 min
Open Questions & Next Steps
Anything unresolved gets documented. We confirm the 30/60/90 check-in cadence and assign any pre-build action items.
04
What This Session Is Not

We want to set clear expectations so nobody walks in surprised. Here's what will not happen in this session:

  • ✕We will not ask for JDE credentials, database access, or system logins. That happens later, under a separate technical access plan.
  • ✕We will not make any architectural commitments or promise specific AI features beyond what is scoped in the SOW.
  • ✕We will not demo a finished product. The discovery session informs what gets built — it comes before the build.
  • ✕We will not require any technical preparation from your team. If you can speak to how your business works today, you're ready.
  • ✕We will not conduct a lengthy audit of your JDE configuration. We need enough context to scope the data replication — not an exhaustive inventory.
05
What to Bring or Prepare

No formal prep is required — but the following inputs will dramatically improve the quality of the session. Even rough answers are better than none.

For the Business / Process Owner

  • 2–3 examples of real data requests fielded in the last 30 days — the messier and more manual, the better
  • A rough sense of frequency: "we get this question 10 times a week"
  • Any existing reports, exports, or dashboards currently used to answer those questions
  • A gut-check on how long it currently takes to fulfill a typical data request

For the JDE / IT Administrator

  • JDE modules in active use (e.g., Sales Order Management, Accounts Receivable, Address Book)
  • Whether any non-standard or custom fields are used for key data (customer names, account codes, etc.)
  • Any known data quality issues or tables that are not reliably maintained
  • Rough sense of data volume — order of magnitude is fine (thousands vs. millions of records)

For the Executive Sponsor

  • The 1–2 business outcomes you most want to demonstrate to leadership from this POC
  • Any internal timeline pressures (board reviews, budget cycles) we should be aware of
  • Names of the stakeholders who will evaluate the POC results at sign-off

For All Attendees

  • No slides, no formal presentations — come ready to have a conversation
  • It's okay to say "I don't know" — we'll note it and follow up
  • If you have a specific frustration with the current process, bring it — that's often where the highest-value use cases live
06
What Comes Out of This Session

Within a few days of the discovery session, ThinkWork will share a documented summary of what we captured. This becomes the shared source of truth for the entire POC engagement.

Deliverables from the Discovery Session

  • Documented baseline — current process, time costs, and manual effort quantified
  • Prioritized use case list — the specific questions the AI chatbot will be tested against
  • Agreed KPIs — the measurable criteria that define POC success at sign-off
  • Stakeholder map — who owns what, who approves UAT, and who receives the executive summary
  • 30/60/90 check-in schedule — when we'll meet to review progress and surface blockers early
  • Open items log — anything that requires follow-up before the build begins

All of this is captured in ThinkWork's Discovery & KPI Tracker — a live document your team will have access to throughout the engagement so there are no surprises at sign-off.
